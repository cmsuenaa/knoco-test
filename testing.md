AERA SKINCARE: ANNUAL REPORT 2025 [AERA Skincare International Private Co.]

DEPARTMENT PERFORMANCE

Finance Department

Fiscal Year 2025 has yielded the strongest financial metrics in AERA's 40-year history. While global economic crosswinds have forced contractions across the broader cosmetics sector, our specific focus on functional value has generated exceptional revenue acceleration. By keeping packaging expenditures below 4% of total cost of goods sold (COGS)—compared to an industry average of 22%—our gross margins reached record efficiency.
    Global Revenue: €184.5M (+26%)
    Gross Profit Margin: 74.2%
    Net Operating Income: €41.8M
  Regional revenue breakdown indicates sustained dominance in Europe, balanced by explosive growth within APAC markets. Specifically, our newly established distribution nodes in Tokyo and Sydney have achieved profitability six months ahead of projection, fueled by an enthusiastic consumer adoption of our functional aesthetic. Capital expenditure was deliberately directed toward scaling R&D laboratory infrastructure and acquiring sustainable logistics assets.

Distributions & Supply Chain

The Distributions department achieved monumental efficiency milestones in 2025, overcoming complex global maritime bottlenecks. To optimize cross-continental logistics, the team finalized localized manufacturing contracts within the APAC zone, eliminating the carbon footprint and import duties associated with shipping heavy bulk products from Europe.
    Ocean Freight Reliance: FY2024: 72% | FY2025: 41% | YoY Shift: -31% (Decarbonization)
    Local APAC Assembly Speed: FY2024: 22 Days | FY2025: 11 Days | YoY Shift: +50% Efficiency Rate
    Packaging Volumetric Waste: FY2024: 14% | FY2025: 1.2% | YoY Shift: Near-Zero Optimization
    Fulfillment Accuracy: FY2024: 98.4% | FY2025: 99.7% | YoY Shift: +1.3% Operational Excellence
  By introducing our standardized bulk liquid transport system, raw product concentrates are now transferred to regional hubs in reusable high-capacity containers, where they are packaged directly into local consumer-facing aluminum or glass vessels. This structural change eliminated over 40 tons of secondary transit packaging material.
