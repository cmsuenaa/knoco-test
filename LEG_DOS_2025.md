# Regional Regulatory Dossiers (EMA, PMDA, TGA)
**Document Ref:** AERA-LEG-DOS-2025

**Department:** Legal and Compliance

**Status:** Multi-Jurisdictional Regulatory Submission Framework

## 1. Executive Summary of Global Product Approvals
*This framework outlines the compliance requirements for AERA’s expanding international product matrix across varying sovereign cosmetic and therapeutic substance categories.*

Product formulations must simultaneously satisfy European, Japanese, and Australian health and safety legislation.

## 2. Jurisdictional Breakdown & Compliance Mandates
European Union (EMA / Cosmetic Regulation EC No 1223/2009): Formulations must be logged directly into the Cosmetic Products Notification Portal (CPNP). Product Information Files (PIF) must document the 40-year safety profile data, stabilization parameters, and complete consumer exposure assessments.

Japan (PMDA / Pharmaceutical and Medical Devices Act): Products must be categorized and registered under the strict "Cosmeceutical" or "Quasi-Drug" classification framework. Formulations must clearly separate active, functional raw botanical ingredients from base vehicle liquids for rigorous verification.

Australia (TGA / Therapeutic Goods Act 1989): Functional topical items making clinical dermal barrier recovery claims must be registered on the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG). Product labels must fully comply with all Therapeutic Goods Advertising Code standards.

## 3. Cross-Border Scientific Dossier Harmonization
Unified Master Dossier: To streamline regional registrations, Legal maintains a single Unified Master Dossier containing all toxicological data, stability testing logs, and clinical trial sheets.

Local Agent Designation: Legal ensures that a certified regional compliance agent is legally registered within Tokyo, Singapore, and Sydney to handle any sudden regulatory inquiries or cosmetic market audits.
